Kedarnath Temple

Kedarnath Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is located in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand, India, near the Mandakini River. The temple is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas and is considered one of the holiest shrines in Hinduism.

The temple is situated at an altitude of 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) above sea level and is surrounded by breathtaking mountain scenery. It is a prominent pilgrimage site for devout Hindus, especially during the Char Dham Yatra.

Visitors to Kedarnath Temple often trek through challenging terrain to reach the temple, making it a spiritually and physically rewarding journey. The temple's architecture is simple yet elegant, and it has a unique spiritual aura.
